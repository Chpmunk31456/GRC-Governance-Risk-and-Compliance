# GRC Practical Manuals

## Governance, Risk, Compliance, Resilience, and Security

A free, practical, and beginner-friendly collection of Governance, Risk, and Compliance manuals for students, junior analysts, managers, cybersecurity professionals, auditors, consultants, and organizations.

**Author:** Alberto (Al) Leiva

---

## About This Repository

The GRC Practical Manuals collection provides clear and practical guidance for understanding, implementing, documenting, testing, and improving cybersecurity governance, risk, compliance, privacy, assurance, cloud security, and operational resilience programs.

The manuals are designed to help readers move beyond theory and understand how GRC work is performed in real organizations.

Each manual may include:

- Plain-language explanations
- Framework and regulatory requirements
- Practical implementation guidance
- Control examples
- Evidence-collection guidance
- Audit-readiness practices
- Risk-assessment methods
- Policies and procedures
- Open-source tools
- Checklists and templates
- Career guidance for junior professionals
- Guidance for managers and program leaders

# How to Use This Repository

1. Select the framework, regulation, or risk area relevant to your needs.
2. Open the corresponding folder.
3. Select either the PDF or Microsoft Word edition.
4. Review the concepts, controls, examples, and implementation guidance.
5. Adapt the material to your organization’s systems, risks, and obligations.
6. Verify requirements using current official sources.
7. Document implementation and retain appropriate evidence.
8. Test controls and review the program regularly.
9. Record weaknesses and corrective actions.
10. Continue improving the program over time.

# Educational Mission

I want people to understand why this work matters so deeply to me.

I was born in 1956. As a Latino man with learning disabilities, I could easily have been underestimated, overlooked, or denied opportunities. I was fortunate to have mentors throughout my life who recognized my potential, encouraged me, and helped me find paths that I might never have discovered on my own.

Their guidance made it possible for me to build a career in technology, education, counseling, rehabilitation engineering, cybersecurity, and service to others. I have never forgotten what they did for me.

That is why I am committed to creating free manuals, career roadmaps, internship and fellowship directories, accessibility resources, and practical technology education.

Many people have the ability to make a meaningful difference in the world, but they lack money, guidance, accommodations, connections, or a clear path forward.

These resources are my way of helping make that path easier to find.
